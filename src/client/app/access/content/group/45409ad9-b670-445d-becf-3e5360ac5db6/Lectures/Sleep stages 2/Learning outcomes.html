After Lecture 3 students will be able to
  1. distinguish NREM and REM sleep stages based on polysomnography and associated physiological parameters;
  2. consider similarities and differences in REM sleep between humans and laboratory animals;
  3. demonstrate knowledge of homeostatic and circadian regulation of REM sleep;
  4. interpret changes in sleep structure based on abnormal regulation of NREM and REM sleep
  5. evaluate the existing theories for REM sleep function;
  6. discuss the relationship between REM sleep and dreaming.
